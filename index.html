Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 July 2026 · Effective: 14 July 2026

Apayo does not collect your personal data. There are no accounts, no sign-up, and no tracking. Everything you enter — your health profile, reminders, scan history — stays on your device and is never sent to us or to anyone else.

01Who we are

Apayo ("the app", "we", "us") is a medicine-information and translation app that helps foreign travelers and residents understand Korean over-the-counter medicines. This policy explains what the app does and does not do with information, and applies to the Apayo iOS application.

02Information we collect

None sent to our servers

Apayo has no user accounts and asks for no name, email, phone number, or login. We do not operate analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs. We do not build a profile of you.

The following information is created and stored only on your device, and we never receive it:

  • Health profile — optional toggles you set (e.g. pregnancy, allergies, conditions) used to show personalized medicine warnings. Stored locally on your phone.
  • Reminders — dose reminders you create, delivered as local notifications on your device.
  • Scan activity — medicines you scan or view. Processed on your device; not uploaded.

03Camera & photos

With your permission, Apayo uses your camera (or a photo you choose) to read the text on a medicine box. This text recognition runs entirely on your device using Apple's on-device Vision framework. Images are not uploaded, stored on a server, or shared. The app only keeps what it needs in memory to identify the medicine, and you can revoke camera access at any time in iOS Settings.

04Medicine data & network requests

Medicine information in Apayo comes from the public open data of the Ministry of Food and Drug Safety (MFDS, 식약처) of the Republic of Korea. Most of this data is bundled inside the app. For a small number of less common medicines, the app may request label information in real time from the Korean public data portal.

These requests contain only the medicine identifier being looked up. They do not include any personal information about you — no identity, no location, no health profile.

05Location

Apayo's pharmacy guides show a fixed, curated list of pharmacies. The app does not track your GPS location or share your location with anyone. If a future version offers location-based pharmacy search, it will ask for permission first and this policy will be updated.

06How your information is used

Because your information stays on your device, it is used only to make the app work for you:

  • To show medicine warnings relevant to your health profile.
  • To deliver the dose reminders you set.
  • To identify medicines you scan.

We do not sell, rent, or share any information, because we do not have it.

07Children

Apayo is a general-audience medical-information app and is not directed at children under 13. It does not knowingly collect information from anyone.

08Your choices

  • Deleting the app removes all locally stored data (profile, reminders, history) from your device.
  • You can turn camera or notification permissions on or off at any time in iOS Settings.
  • You can clear your health profile inside the app at any time.

09Not medical advice

Apayo provides general information to help overcome the language barrier. It is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Always consult a pharmacist or doctor before taking any medicine.

10Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles information, we will update this page and revise the "Last updated" date above. Material changes will be reflected in the app.
